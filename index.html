About

Hello, world: I'm Lucrezia!

I am a molecular biotechnologist interested in Stem Cell & Developmental Biology. Currently, I work as a PhD candidate in the Cell Biology of Tissue Morphogenesis laboratory, i.e. Norden lab, at the Instituto Gulbenkian de CiÃªncia.

My main research interest is to understand how progenitor stem cells can integrate intrinsic and extrinisc signals to coordinate different programs during development, such as proliferation and differentiation. To this end, I am studying a particular epithelium, called psuedostratified, which is often found as organ precursors in many developmental contexts across evolution. In particular, I am trying to understand the role of pseudostratification in organogenesis of the vertebrate retina, using zebrafish as a model system.

To find more about my research and my interests, check out the Projects and Publication sections!
